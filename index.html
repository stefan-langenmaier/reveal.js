Virtualisation vs Containerisation

What is the difference? Running a different kernel with maybe a different configuration? Wider or narrower: my SYSVIPC problem for exemple with full disk-encryption LUKS and apache might be better to isolate different images together with their kernels to have _more_ reproduciable containers

Full virtualised systems all run on their on kernels. Moderen hardware has support for it. Paravirtualised systems run on their on kernel, but the kernel knows its running virtualised and is optimized for that. Containerized systems run all on the same kernel. So this kernel has a global ressource control and can do a global optimzation of the resources. Possibly big performance gains now and in the future. Competing environments can "talk" to each other. So generally a good idea to use containers? Well it depends, as you exclude your kernel from the image your behaviour can change in the future when you update your kernel or when you change your distribution.

Containerization differences/alternatives

What are the differences? Why do they exist? OpenVZ Linux VServer they are only available via patches not part of the official kernel docker lxd lxc lmctfy rocket systemd-nsspawn they are all based on cgroups, which is in the vanilla kernel. so easy upgrades in the future very important point, as all your containers depend on that kernel jails zones similair things for other oses
